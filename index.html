[ About ]

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Communication Science at the University of Amsterdam. My research employs methodologies including experiments, surveys, and neuroimaging (fMRI) to study the neuropsychological mechanisms underlying polarization. I put specific focus on individual-level biased information handling behaviors, including selective sharing, biased interpretation (motivated reasoning), and selective consumption.

On the behavioral and psychological level, I'm interested in how individual and group-level pathways shape information handling decisions concurrently. On the neural level, I'm interested in how cognitive and emotional processes interplay and how they are implemented in human brains, giving rise to higher-level, complex social behaviors.

In the future, I'm interested in extending this knowledge into interventions to effectively de-bias social behaviors online, in different contexts including but not limited to political, environmental, public health, and more.
